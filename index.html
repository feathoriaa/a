Poetic Spotlight

"I said: what about my eyes? He said: Keep them on the road.
I said: What about my passion? He said: Keep it burning.
I said: What about my heart? He said: Tell me what you hold inside it?
I said: Pain and sorrow. He said: Stay with it. The wound is the place where the Light enters you." — Rumi


"No man is an island,
Entire of itself,
Every man is a piece of the continent,
A part of the main." — John Donne


"Shall I compare thee to a summer’s day?
Thou art more lovely and more temperate:
Rough winds do shake the darling buds of May,
And summer’s lease hath all too short a date;" — William Shakespeare


"I do not love you except because I love you;
I go from loving to not loving you,
From waiting to not waiting for you
My heart moves from cold to fire." — Pablo Neruda


"Do not stand at my grave and weep
I am not there. I do not sleep.
I am a thousand winds that blow.
I am the diamond glints on snow." — Mary Elizabeth Frye


"The tulips are too red in the first place, they hurt me.
Even through the gift paper I could hear them breathe
Lightly, through the white walls of my room." — Sylvia Plath


"Some say the world will end in fire,
Some say in ice.
From what I’ve tasted of desire
I hold with those who favor fire." — Robert Frost


"You may write me down in history
With your bitter, twisted lies,
You may tread me in the very dirt
But still, like dust, I'll rise." — Maya Angelou


"Hold fast to dreams
For if dreams die
Life is a broken-winged bird
That cannot fly." — Langston Hughes


"Time present and time past
Are both perhaps present in time future,
And time future contained in time past." — T.S. Eliot


"Tell me not, in mournful numbers,
Life is but an empty dream!
For the soul is dead that slumbers,
And things are not what they seem." — Henry Wadsworth Longfellow

If you can trust yourself when all men doubt you,
But make allowance for their doubting too:" — Rudyard Kipling
